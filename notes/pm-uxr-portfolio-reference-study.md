# PM + UXR Portfolio Reference Study

Date: 2026-04-24

## Goal

Reposition the portfolio for Product Manager and User Research applications while keeping the site polished and visually led.

## What Strong PM Portfolios Emphasize

- Lead with context and result: role, product area, one-line decision, and the strongest outcome should appear early.
- Show product thinking, not only deliverables: explain the problem, why it mattered, the options considered, and what tradeoffs shaped the direction.
- Make constraints visible: timeline, scope, available data, technical limits, and what was intentionally deprioritized.
- Results matter more than mockups: even when metrics are conceptual or qualitative, clearly state what changed, what was validated, and what the next step would be.
- Keep case studies skimmable: overview, problem, process, solution, results, and learning should be readable in a few minutes.

## What Strong UXR Portfolios Emphasize

- Research portfolios do not need to be flashy; hiring teams look for research plans, reports, personas, user-need statements, journey maps, and other research-based artifacts.
- The story should connect research to product decisions: what was learned, how it changed direction, and what recommendation came out of the study.
- A UXR case study should focus on user motivations and problems, plus the effect of research on design or product decisions.
- Visuals should make evidence easier to scan: journey maps, survey snapshots, method cards, synthesis diagrams, and annotated decision flows.

## Direction for This Portfolio

- Position Yixiao as an evidence-led product thinker: PM + UX Research + UX Design.
- Replace generic design language with product/research language:
  - "What decision did this support?"
  - "What user evidence shaped the direction?"
  - "What tradeoff was made?"
  - "What outcome or learning did it produce?"
- Keep visuals, but make them support product reasoning:
  - Final UI = product direction
  - Flow maps = product logic
  - Generated visuals = atmosphere only
  - Cards = evidence, decision, tradeoff, outcome

## Implementation Checklist

- Section labels should shift from design-process language to PM/UXR language.
- About page should emphasize research methods, product judgment, and cross-functional fit.
